Fuel-Saving Tips
Practical ways to stretch every liter in Philippine road conditions.
🚀
Maintain 80–90 km/h on expressways
The sweet spot for most PH vehicles. Wind resistance increases exponentially at higher speeds. At 110 km/h you burn up to 25% more than at 90 km/h.
Save up to 25%
🔧
Check tire pressure every week
Each PSI drop below recommended pressure increases fuel consumption by ~0.5%. In PH heat, tires deflate faster — check in the morning when cold.
Save up to 10%
🌡️
Pre-cool before entering on hot days
Open doors and let hot air escape before turning on A/C. A cabin at 50°C needs 5–8 min of full-blast A/C, consuming extra fuel for that cooldown.
Save 5–8%
🛑
Avoid aggressive braking & acceleration
Smooth driving is key. Hard acceleration from stops wastes fuel. Anticipate traffic flow — lifting off the throttle early uses engine braking for free.
Save 15–20%
🕐
Travel off-peak (before 6:30AM or after 8PM)
EDSA and major PH roads have severe stop-and-go traffic during rush hour. Idling & frequent stops can triple your fuel use compared to free-flowing conditions.
Save up to 40%
🔄
Change air filter every 10,000–15,000 km
A clogged air filter restricts airflow to the engine, causing it to run richer (more fuel). In dusty PH roads, filters clog faster than the service interval suggests.
Save 6–10%
❄️
Use A/C wisely — windows open below 60 km/h
At low city speeds, open windows are more efficient. At highway speeds above 60 km/h, A/C beats open windows due to increased drag.
Save 3–7%
⛽
Don't top off — fill to 3/4 tank
A lighter vehicle uses less fuel. Carrying a full 45L tank vs 33L adds ~10 kg of weight. On short city trips this matters more than you think.
Minor savings
🛣️
Use expressways even with toll fees
SLEX/NLEX tollways often save more in fuel than they cost in tolls. Avoiding EDSA on a 40 km trip can save ₱50–₱120 in fuel alone vs alternative routes.
Net savings possible
